Addictiveness of video games

How addictive are they?


Video games are highly addictive for a small percent of people. So much so that there is now an official dissorder called "internet gaming disorder". While only a 3-4% of people get internet gaming disorder, there is a wider effect. Even if a person doesn't get highly addicted to a video game, that doesn't mean that they are not still addicted. Around 7% of gamers suffer from slight addiction to video games, which could lead into internet gaming disorder.


The side effects of internet gaming disorder are: isolation from friends and family, constant thought on the game and sadness or irritation when not playing the game.

Why are they addictive?


Video games are addictive because they force your brain into releasing a chemical called dopamine. Dopamine is the hormone that is given to your brain to show it
